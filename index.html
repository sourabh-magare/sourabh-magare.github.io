I'm a PhD candidate at the Inter-University Centre for Astronomy and Astrophysics (IUCAA), Pune, India, expecting to graduate in August 2026. My research sits at the crossroads of gravitational wave physics, gravitational lensing, and multi-messenger astronomy.

I am pioneering a novel early warning systemstrong> for multi-messenger astronomy using strong gravitational lensing. By calculating time delays between lensed images of BNS/NSBH host galaxiesstrong>, my approach predicts the 'action replay'strong> of an event to capture pre-merger electromagnetic counterparts hours to days in advancestrong>.

As an active member of the LIGO Scientific Collaboration, I developed pipeline — SLICK (Strong Lensing Identification of Candidates Kindred) — that use deep learning to identify strongly lensed gravitational wave pairs in real-time detector data. SLICK is currently integrated into the LVK O4b analysis pipeline.

Beyond detection, I worked on kilonova observability from binary neutron star and neutron star–black hole mergers, forecasting massive black hole binaries with LISA using the NINJA cosmological simulations.

Gravitational Wave Lensing

Pioneering a novel early warning system for multi-messenger astronomy using strong gravitational lensing. By calculating time delays between lensed images of BNS/NSBH host galaxies, this approach predicts the 'action replay' of an event to capture pre-merger electromagnetic counterparts hours to days in advance.

💥

Multi-Messenger Astronomy

Modeling kilonova light curves from NSBH mergers using NMMA and MOSFiT frameworks. Investigating electromagnetic counterpart observability across LSST bands and developing strategies for rapid follow-up of gravitational wave events.

🕳️

Massive Black Hole Binaries

Forecasting LISA-detectable massive black hole binary mergers using the NINJA cosmological simulation suite. Characterizing merger rates, mass distributions, and multi-band gravitational wave detection prospects.

🧠

Machine Learning in Astrophysics

Building physics-inspired spatiotemporal graph neural networks (LensedGW-STGNN) for pairwise lensing detection. Applying deep learning architectures as fast first-pass filters for gravitational wave data analysis pipelines.
